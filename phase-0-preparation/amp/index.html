Mission Guatemala Phase 0

Objective:

Send 15-20 fully functional laptops to Guatemala to provide doctors and students with medical software and coding / programming resources.

Implementation:

To avoid the possibility of taxes, tariffs, confiscation, suspicion, or questioning from the Guatemalan customs (all possible outcomes from bringing one luggage full of laptops across international borders)

The plan is to use the high school mission trip (Phase 1) as a cover to get these laptops across the border.

Laptops will be distributed to each attendee of the trip before take-off. Students bringing 1-2 laptops shouldn't raise any eyebrows.

Once in Guatemala, laptops will be configured for their purpose.

Purpose:

There are 2 main reasons behind getting these laptops to Guatemala.

  1. Doctors 👨‍⚕️👩‍⚕️
    The Guatemalan doctors need personal computers to stay connected with other doctors in the medical network. Currently, very few doctors have a desktop computer let alone a laptop.
  2. Students 👨‍🎓👩‍🎓
    Phase 2 involves teaching Guatemalan children / students how to code, program, and increase technical awareness and know-how. (Potentially alongside the Guatemala Literacy Project at Cooperative for Education)
    The premise behind phase 2 is to merely expose the Guatemalan students to programming and give them the opportunity and resources to learn. (Just like I was given as a freshmen in college)
    The computers in the computer lab (see photo) may not currently be operable and laptops would provide students the ability to learn away from the computer lab.
