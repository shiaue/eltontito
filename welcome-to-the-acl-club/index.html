Welcome to the (A)CLub

When the journey ahead seems bleak, don't forget to look behind you and see all you have survived already

If you are reading this... Either you've had your ACL repaired or will have your ACL repaired.
... Or you are one of my blessed friends with strong ACLs üí™

While there is definitely a long road ahead, I can assure you it is not a road less traveled. (Check out the official ACL Club)


For those on the rehab journey or about to embark on one...

Here are some of my PRO TIPS:

1. Do NOT compare your recovery timeline with that of others.

  • Teddy Roosevelt said that "Comparison is the thief of joy" and in this case it is also the thief of a healthy and stable recovery as well.
  • Yes, Adrian Peterson was running in 12 weeks and doing NFL level workouts in 5 months... but not all knees were created equally. Comparing my timeline with AP's does nothing...
  • I don't have his surgery, rehabilitation protocol, body composition, and 24/7 training staff.

2. Set rehab goals for yourself

  • Just because you aren't comparing to others doesn't mean you can't set goals for your rehabilitation
  • Tell yourself you are going to increase your ROM by X degrees this week
    • Do those leg raises without the brace
    • Walk a lap on the track
    • Single leg balance for the first time

3. Don't think of PAIN as a "bad" thing

  • Especially when you are cranking those heel slides out... remember how crucial ROM is in your life.
  • If you want full ROM again, just keep pulling

4. Redefine Your Definition of "Failure"

  • Failure is not lifting less weight than you used to lift when you were healthy
  • Failure is not running / walking / moving slower than you did pre-op
  • Let's be honest here, the only real failure is when you don't feel like doing your PT exercises or start dwelling on the things that your body currently cannot physically do.

5. Track Your Progress

  • Regardless if it's a diary, journal, blog, ... etc.
    What's important is that you are tracking your journey
  • This allows you to look back and reflect on the lessons you learned from rehab (I know you learned something)
  • This also allows you to see how far you've come (And boy is it amazing to see your strength double in nearly a week)

6. Change How You Measure Success

  • Stop measuring your "success" by what you can or can't do. (Somethings take longer to do than others)
  • Measure your success by effort, it's something you have 100% control over

7. Attitude is EVERYTHING

  • Studies have shown that a bad attitude affects more aspects of your live than just your rehab
  • Here are 3 sources that back me up
    1. TED Talk
    2. Can Negative Thinking Make You Sick?
    3. A Negative Attitude Affects Every Aspect Of Your Life
  • If you fully believe that today you will be at least 1% stronger than you were yesterday, what's stopping you from doing just that.

8. Learn Something New

  • What better time to start something new then when life throws you a curveball.
  • Suggestions:
    • Reading (non-academic, books you want to read... yes, those do exist)
    • Biking (This is the first high intensity cardio you will most likely be cleared to do)
    • Swimming (Once you are cleared to swim, it's no impact and great cardio)
    • Learn an instrument (As long as it doesn't require your recovering leg)
    • Learn a new language (You can learn almost any language online now, check out my favorite site, Duolingo)
  • Take time to enjoy something that you wouldn't have done if you didn't get hurt. Because that way, you will have learned / done something that without your injury you would have never learned.

9. Celebrate the Little Victories (Following up on #2.)

  • As it should be with anything in life, be sure to enjoy and celebrate the little achievements.

    Whether it be a few more degrees of ROM, or your first time walking without crutches / brace, or even your first pain-free lap around the track...
    A win is a win.

10. Practice Gratitude

  • I wholeheartedly believe gratitude is the only way to open the door to true joy and happiness
  • If you don't take my word for it, here are some TED Talks that reveal the truth and power behind gratitude

"Life changing gratitude does not fasten to a life unless nailed through with one very specific nail at a time"

"The whole of the life -- even the hard -- is made up of the minute parts, and if I miss the infinitesimals, I miss the whole. These are new language lessons, and I live them out. There is a way to live the big of giving thanks in all things. It is this: to give thanks in this one small thing. The moments will add up."
Ann Voskamp
